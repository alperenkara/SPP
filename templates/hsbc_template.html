Think, before you click - don't be tricked.

Hello ,

 

Life moves at a fast pace. Many of us are doing multiple things at once, often using tools intended to make tasks more convenient, like online shopping and banking.

 

Fraudsters and scammers operate in this space, capitalising on the speed of your actions. That’s why you always need to be on the lookout to protect yourself from fraud. Treat unexpected emails, texts and calls as suspicious. Don’t click on links in emails or texts and never give personal information on a cold call. Fraudsters will try and create a sense of urgency, but don’t panic, take your time. If it feels wrong or too good to be true, it often is.

 

Think before you click.

If it’s an unexpected email, text, or call, treat it as suspicious. Stop and think.

Clicking on a link is like letting a stranger into your home. Don’t click it, delete it.

If in doubt, check with a family member or friend before taking action.

Report any suspicious activity, it builds awareness of fraud and helps everybody.

If you’re making a purchase on online, review the seller and pay by card for extra protection.

Making an investment? Research the company and check they’re FCA regulated.
